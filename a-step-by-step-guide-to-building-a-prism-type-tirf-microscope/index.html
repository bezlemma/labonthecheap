A step-by-step guide to building a prism-type TIRF microscope

October 11, 2019


Total Internal Reflection Florescence (TIRF) Microscopy is a technique used to observe a very thin region of sample directly at the surface of an interface (coverslip) without obtaining background interference from the rest of the sample volume.



Dalton Gibbs, Anisa Kaur, Anoja Megalathan, Kumar Sapkota, and Soma Dhakal have published a wonderful paper entitled "Build Your Own Microscope: Step-By-Step Guide for Building a Prism-Based TIRF Microscope".



As the title implies, it is a step-by-step guide to build a prism-based TIRF microscope. It includes a bill of materials, step-by-step instructions, software links, beautiful diagrams and a level of technical detail that goes far beyond just how to build the device. We love to see this kind of thing.



The bill of materials doesn't have any prices, so we thought we'd put together as many specific prices as we could here, which come to a total of around $30,000. This includes everything and we do mean everything, from the table to the optical glasses. You can save money in a lot of ways by swapping out parts like the shutters.
